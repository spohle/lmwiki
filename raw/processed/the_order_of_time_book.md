# The Crumbling of Time (Carlo Rovelli)

In Carlo Rovelli’s *The Order of Time*, the first section—**"The Crumbling of Time"**—deconstructs our common-sense notion of how time works. He identifies several ways in which the "unified, flowing time" we imagine is actually an illusion.

Here are the five fundamental ways Rovelli explains how time "crumbles":

---

## 1. The Loss of Unity (Time Slows Down)

One of the most famous takeaways is that time does not pass at the same rate everywhere. Einstein’s General Relativity showed that gravity slows time down. If you place one clock on a mountain and another at sea level, the clock closer to the Earth (where gravity is stronger) will tick more slowly.

---

## 2. The Loss of Direction (The "Arrow" of Time)

In the fundamental laws of physics (like those describing the movement of atoms), there is no distinction between past and future. Equations work the same way forward as they do backward.

Rovelli explains that the only law of physics that distinguishes the past from the future is the Second Law of Thermodynamics, which states that heat cannot pass from a cold body to a hot one. Our sense of time flowing forward is essentially just the universe moving from a state of lower entropy to higher entropy.

---

## 3. The Loss of the "Present"

We usually think of the "Now" as something happening everywhere in the universe simultaneously. Rovelli argues that "now" is a localized concept, much like "here."

Because light takes time to travel, there is no way to define a single moment that is "happening" simultaneously on Earth and in a distant galaxy. The notion of a universal "present" is a bubble that only makes sense in our immediate vicinity.

---

## 4. The Loss of Independence

Isaac Newton imagined time as an empty "container" or a straight line that exists even if nothing happens. Rovelli explains that Einstein changed this: time is not independent of the world.

Instead, it is part of the spacetime fabric, which can be warped, stretched, and bent by matter and energy. Time is not the background of the dance; it is part of the dance itself.

---

## 5. The Quanta of Time (Granularity)

Finally, Rovelli introduces concepts from Loop Quantum Gravity. He suggests that time is not continuous or "smooth."

Just as light is made of photons, time has a minimum scale—the Planck time:

\[
t_P \approx 5.4 \times 10^{-44} \text{ seconds}
\]

Below this scale, the concept of time ceases to exist. It is "granular," meaning it consists of discrete "leaps" rather than a flowing stream.
