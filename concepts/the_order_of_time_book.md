Carlo Rovelli's concept, detailed in *[[The Order of Time]]*, argues that our intuitive understanding of time as a single, uniform, flowing entity is an illusion. He outlines five fundamental ways this notion 'crumbles':

### 1. The Loss of Unity (Time Dilation)
*   Time does not pass uniformly across the universe. [[Einstein's General Relativity]] demonstrates that gravity affects the rate at which time passes; clocks closer to a massive object (like Earth) tick more slowly than those further away.

### 2. The Loss of Direction (The Arrow of Time)
*   Fundamental physical laws are generally symmetric, meaning they work equally well running forward or backward in time. 
*   The distinction between past and future arises primarily from the [[Second Law of Thermodynamics]], which dictates that entropy must increase—the universe moves from states of lower to higher entropy.

### 3. The Loss of the "Present"
*   The idea of a universal, simultaneous 'Now' is localized. Because light requires time to travel, there can be no single moment experienced simultaneously across vast cosmic distances; 'now' is relative to an observer's location.

### 4. The Loss of Independence (Spacetime)
*   Contrary to the Newtonian view of time as a static, independent container, Rovelli emphasizes that time is intrinsically woven into the fabric of spacetime. Matter and energy warp and bend this fabric; time is not the background against which events occur—it *is* part of the event.

### 5. The Quanta of Time (Granularity)
*   Drawing from [[Loop Quantum Gravity]], Rovelli suggests that time is not infinitely smooth but possesses a minimum, discrete scale. This fundamental unit is the Planck time ($t_P$):
    $$t_P \approx 5.4 \times 10^{-44} \text{ seconds}$$
*   Below this quantum level, the concept of continuous time breaks down; time exists as discrete 'leaps'.
