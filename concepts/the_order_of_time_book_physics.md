Carlo Rovelli's exploration in his work challenges the classical notion of time as a fundamental, flowing river. Instead, he posits that time is an emergent property arising from complex physical and psychological processes.

### Core Philosophical Shifts
*   **Events over Things:** The universe is fundamentally composed of **events** (e.g., a kiss, a falling stone), not persistent 'objects.' Even what we perceive as a stable object is merely a very long-lived pattern in the field of atoms.
*   **Time as Thermodynamic Effect:** Rovelli proposes the **Thermal Time Hypothesis**, suggesting that time is not intrinsic to reality but arises from heat and the statistical behavior of large systems. A perfectly cold, static universe would lack the sensation of time.

### The Illusion of Flow
The perception of time flowing from past to future—the 'arrow of time'—is explained by our own limitations. Because we cannot track every single molecule in a system, we observe it statistically. This inability to track everything leads to **"blurring,"** which is what generates entropy and gives us the sensation that time is passing.

### Quantum Foundations
At the deepest level of physics, Rovelli points to equations like those formulated by Wheeler and DeWitt ($H\Psi = 0$), which do not contain a time variable ($t$). This suggests that at the most fundamental scale, time is unnecessary for describing reality; it emerges as a secondary effect, much like 'temperature' emerges from molecular motion.

### Identity and Memory
Our sense of self—the persistent 'I' across time—is also an emergent construct. Rovelli argues that personal identity is built entirely upon the traces of memory held within our brains.

### Classical vs. Rovelli's Viewpoint Comparison
| Concept | Classical View | Rovelli's View |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Structure | A continuous, flowing river | Granular, discrete "leaps" |
| Foundation | A basic law of the universe | An emergent property of entropy |
| Reality | Made of "things" | Made of "events" |
| The Present | A universal moment | A local, subjective bubble |
